Let's graduate

Finding your major

For some, finding a major that really calls to them can be hard.

Do some research: Degrees at UBC

This is the perfect place to explore multiple pathways. Many people don't know what they want to do going in and others switch in the beginning of their degree. It's normal.

Need help with a class?

UBC provides many different source of help with accomadations to fit your schedule. Reach out. They're here for you.

  • Learning Commons: There are tutors for many first year subjects available in the Irving K. Barber Library. Just check the schedule beforehand and walk in.

  • Math Learning Center: Mondays through fridays, there are graduate students who can assist you with every math problem imaginable.

  • AMS Tutoring: Free tutoring in every first year residence for Math, Biology, Physics, and Chemistry.

What is class like?

Difficult?

Classes taught by a good professor can make it so much more enjoyable as well as help you through difficult parts. Just google your class, major or faculty. There's probably someone talking about it online.

ALWAYS use Rate My Prof before registering for a section. While you won't be able to get the best prof for everything due to scheduling conflicts and availability, you can at least increase the amount of good professors you have while here.

What are classes like?

If you want to get a students perspective on classes, there are many blogs by current students. Just google the class to see if someone has blogged about it.

For example, Sophia runs a great engineering blog. Also, Vincent reviews many classes in his computer science blog

Need specific help?

Almost every student is going to have some questions about their major sometime at university.

Try taking these steps to get help.

  1. Most basic questions about planning courses can be answered by looking at the course outline for your intended major. Try a quick google search first.
  2. Every faculty has advisors on staff that you can meet with in person. Find your faculty.
  3. Each student is assigned a specific advisor that you can meet with one on one. If you are completely lost, a meeting with them will help a lot
