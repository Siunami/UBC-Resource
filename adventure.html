Adventure

Amazing experiences all year round

Find something fun to do.

Hit the snow.

With multiple mountains nearby and all of them offering student passes , there's no reason not to go.

Whistler : About 3 hours away, Whistler is a premier destination for snow enthusiasts around the world.

There is also a bus service that can take you up and back for relatively cheap. Epic Rides

Grouse : A small resort that is only 2 hours away by bus. Recommended for those on a budget or just learning.

Cypress : A decent sized resort with two medium sized mountains. Intermediate and beginner terrain.

Cypress also has a couple buses that pick up from multiple locations in Vancouver. Cypress Coach Lines

Seymour : Another small resort that is great for those just learning.

Get some fresh air.

There are so many great hiking destinations in the Vancouver area to keep you exploring.

Nearby

Pacific Spirit Regional Park: A forest bordering the UBC campus. It's easy to feel like you are in another world while there.

Take a bus

Grouse Grind: An intense uphill ascent north of campus. The view of Vancouver from the top is absolutely stunning. On a sunny day, bring friends and food for a picnic.

Lynn Canyon: Featuring a suspension bridge, multiple hiking trails and swimming holes.

By car

Garibaldi Lake: A turqouise colored lake with alpine mountains in the backdrop. Truly spectacular.

Panorama Ridge: 15 km to the top to get a panorama view (haha get it?) of the surrounding mountains and lakes.

Hot Springs: British Columbia is home to many scenic and relaxing hotsprings.


There are so many more hiking destination for explore. For a more detailed list check out https://www.vancouvertrails.com/.
